# Self-Sovereign Identity

Self-sovereign identity (SSI) gives individuals control over their digital identities without relying on centralized authorities. SSI enables privacy-preserving authentication, verifiable credentials, and decentralized identity management.

## Implementations

### walt.id Identity Toolkit
[walt-id/waltid-identity](https://github.com/walt-id/waltid-identity) - All-in-one open-source identity and wallet toolkit. Comprehensive solution for building SSI applications with support for decentralized identifiers (DIDs), verifiable credentials, and standards-compliant identity wallets. Built with Kotlin and designed for production-grade implementations of self-sovereign identity systems.

### Credo TypeScript Framework
[openwallet-foundation/credo-ts](https://github.com/openwallet-foundation/credo-ts) - TypeScript framework for building decentralized identity and verifiable credential solutions. Part of the OpenWallet Foundation, providing a robust SDK for implementing SSI protocols and standards in JavaScript/TypeScript applications.

### CREDEBL Platform
[credebl/platform](https://github.com/credebl/platform) - Open source, open standards-based decentralized identity and verifiable credentials platform. Built with TypeScript, provides enterprise-ready infrastructure for issuing, holding, and verifying credentials in compliance with SSI standards.

### Procivis One Core
[procivis/one-core](https://github.com/procivis/one-core) - Issue, hold and verify digital identities and credentials on any device with eIDAS 2.0 compliancy, ISO 18013-5 mdocs, IETF SD-JWT VC, OID4VC, and W3C VCs. A comprehensive Rust implementation supporting multiple international standards for digital identity.
